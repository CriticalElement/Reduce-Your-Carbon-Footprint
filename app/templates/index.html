How to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

The NOAA estimates that by 2050 sea levels worldwide will rise by 2 to 3 feet. That might just seem like a small number, but the impact this will have on our world is unprecedented. Portions of many costal cities will go underwater, and millions of people living in river deltas will have to abandon their agricultural lands and evacuate. The underlying cause of this problem is global warming caused by increasing CO2 amounts in our atmosphere. To stop this problem, we have to reduce each of our carbon footprints.

Click the following to learn more about what is a carbon footprint, why we need to reduce our carbon footprints, and how to reduce your carbon footprint.

What is a carbon fooprint?

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases created by an individual, and these gases are often a consqueunce of using fossil fuels like natural gas and coal. These greenhouse gases trap the sun's heat in the air and contribute to global warming. The average person in the United States has an estimated carbon footprint of 16 tons, which is much higher than the global average of 4 tons per person. These high carbon footprints show that CO2 in the atmosphere is increasing at a steady rate, causing global warming and many other negative effects.

Now that you know what a carbon fooptrint is, learn why you need to reduce yours.

Why do I need to reduce my carbon footprint?

Increasing CO2 in our atmosphere will lead to a multitude of problems, including rising sea levels, climate change, and global warming. In addition to these, the greenhouse gasses in the air cause extreme weather, such as wildfires and droughts. These problems are just a few out of many that will drastically change our society in the next 50 to 100 years and beyond, but they have some of the most severe consequences. To stop this, everyone can make just a few small changes to their lifestyle.

Now that you know why you need to reduce your carbon footprint, learn how to reduce yours.

How do I reduce my carbon footprint?

Use Cars Less

Cars release a huge amount of CO2 into the atmosphere. By simply using cars less, you can make a huge impact on your carbon footprint.
